Smart Financial Tips for Borrowers

Pay Larger Down Payment

A higher down payment reduces your principal amount, lowering your EMI and total interest paid.

Choose Shorter Tenure

While EMIs might be higher, a shorter loan tenure drastically cuts down the total interest outgo.

Make Part Payments

Use bonuses or extra savings to make part payments to reduce your outstanding principal.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. Equated monthly installments are used to pay off both interest and principal each month so that over a specified number of years, the loan is paid off in full.

How is EMI calculated?

The mathematical formula for calculating EMI is: EMI = P × r × (1 + r)^n / ((1 + r)^n - 1) where P is Principal loan amount, r is rate of interest calculated on a monthly basis, and n is loan tenure in months.

What happens if I make part-prepayments?

Making part-prepayments reduces your outstanding principal amount. This directly reduces the interest portion in your future EMIs, either lowering your EMI amount or decreasing the overall loan tenure depending on your bank's policy.

How can I find the best interest rate?

Maintain a good credit score (750+), compare rates across different banks, keep a low debt-to-income ratio, and occasionally negotiate with lenders when you have a strong financial profile.
